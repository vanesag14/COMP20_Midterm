About us

Pastelitos is a small local POC-owned pastry shop in Boston ran by four college students: Vanesa, Marianne, Vanessa, and Daniel. The four co-founder come from diverse backgrounds.

Meet us

Vanesa Gonzalez Torres

Growing up, Vanesa enjoyed drinking a cafecito con un pan dulce in the mornings with her family; it was a tradition. Her tia made the best pan, and now Vanesa is trying to share her tia's recipes to the world.


Vanesa is a junior at Tufts University studying Human Factors Engineering and Computer Science. Though Vanesa is a full time student, she works at Pastelitos to support her college career.

Vanessa Tascon Meneses

When Vanessa was younger, her mother would take her every week to her local pastelito shop, and let her pick out a few sweet treats. Naturally, she developed quite a taste for good Hispanic pastries. When she moved to college from Miami to Boston, missing the Cuban pastries she used to eat as a kid, decided to spend her free time working part time at a Hispanic bakery a few blocks from her school. Eventually, with the help of her friends, she decided to open up her own shop, Pastelitos.


Vanessa is currently studying human factors engineering at tufts university and works part time as Pastelitos. In her free time she enjoys baking, drawing, and painting.
